Introduction

Reinforcement learning has been a popular research area in the field of artificial intelligence. The use of World Models has been a popular approach to solve the problem of model-based reinforcement learning. The project aims to explore the use of Behaviour Cloning Algorithms in World Models. In the following sections, we briefly explain World Models and Behaviour Cloning.

World Models

World models are sample efficient ... but ... Model-based .... (Clear difference between the two) We use an agent called IRIS that trains in a transformer-based world model.

Behaviour Cloning

Behaviour cloning is a simple and effective way to learn a policy from expert demonstrations. It is a supervised learning approach where the agent learns to mimic the expert's actions. The agent is trained on a dataset of expert demonstrations and learns to predict the actions of the expert given the state of the environment.

World Model Architecture

IRIS learn its policy using a World model consisting of an encoder, decoder, and a transformer. The encoder is responsible for encoding the input image observations into a latent space representation. The decoder is responsible for decoding the latent space representation back into the image space. The transformer is responsible for learning the dynamics of the environment by autoregressively producing the next state, reward, and done signal, given the previous states and actions.
